Hanini

Hanini is one of the distinctive popular dishes of the khalijy people, and the origin of the dish comes from the Saudi city of Qassim; where this dish is prepared from the brown flour in addition to the dates. This dish is prepared in the winter and in the month of Ramadan a lot because it contains high-calorie dates that contribute to giving the body the necessary energy and warmth.

Marasei

Marasei, or Masabib or Maraqish, is a popular dish from the Najdi cuisine in Saudi Arabia. It is a snack between main meals. It is prepared from flour, sugar, oil, bread seeds, and vanilla. It is eaten salty when prepared using white or green onions, or sweet with honey and ghee.

Ingredients

  • A cup and a half of warm water
  • A tablespoon of sugar
  • a pinch of salt
  • Two tablespoons of powdered milk
  • Two tablespoons of oil
  • 0.5 teaspoon baking powder
  • A cup and a quarter of whole wheat flour
